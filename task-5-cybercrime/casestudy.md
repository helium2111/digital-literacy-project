# UPI FRAUD CASE STUDY 

A recent and common cybercrime in India involves UPI payment fraud through fake payment requests. In one real-life inspired incident, a college student tried to sell an old phone on an online marketplace. Soon after posting the ad, the student received a message from a buyer who showed interest in purchasing the item.

The fraudster claimed to make an advance payment and sent a QR code, asking the student to scan it to “receive” money. Trusting the buyer, the student scanned the QR code and entered their UPI PIN, assuming it was required to accept the payment. However, instead of receiving money, the student unknowingly authorized a payment from their own account. Within a few minutes, multiple transactions were made, resulting in a significant financial loss.

This type of fraud targets people who are not fully aware of how UPI systems work, especially students and first-time users of online marketplaces. The fraudsters exploit confusion between “request” and “receive” payments and create urgency to avoid suspicion.

The consequences include immediate financial loss and emotional stress. This case highlights the importance of understanding digital payment systems and being cautious while interacting with unknown individuals online.
